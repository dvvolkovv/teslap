## Persona 1: Eva -- The Digital Nomad Expat

### Demographics
- **Age:** 29
- **Location:** Currently Berlin, Germany (originally from Tallinn, Estonia)
- **Occupation:** UX Designer at a remote-first tech company
- **Income:** EUR 55,000/year
- **Languages:** Estonian, English, German (intermediate)
- **Tech Savviness:** High

### Background
Eva moved to Berlin two years ago for the lifestyle but works remotely for a company headquartered in Tallinn. She gets paid in EUR and frequently travels across Europe for both work and pleasure. She previously used a traditional Estonian bank but found it too slow and expensive for her multi-country lifestyle. She switched to Revolut for daily spending but still maintains a traditional bank account for her salary deposits because she does not fully trust neobanks with her main funds.

### Goals
- Have a single banking app that handles all her financial needs across EU borders
- Minimize FX fees when traveling to non-eurozone countries (Poland, Sweden, Czech Republic)
- Instant transfers to friends and family in Estonia and across the EU
- A physical card that works everywhere with no foreign transaction fees

### Pain Points
- Traditional banks charge EUR 5-15 per international transfer
- Her current neobank sometimes declines transactions abroad with no clear reason
- KYC re-verification processes are tedious and invasive
- Cannot hold multiple currencies in her traditional bank account

### TeslaPay Relevance
- Multi-currency accounts solve her cross-border lifestyle needs
- EU-regulated (Lithuania) gives her confidence vs. offshore alternatives
- Instant SEPA payments cover her main payment corridor
- Mastercard acceptance is universal in her travel destinations

### Key Scenarios
1. Receives salary in EUR, converts portion to PLN for upcoming Warsaw trip
2. Sends EUR 200 to her mother in Tallinn via instant SEPA
3. Pays for coworking space in Prague using Mastercard contactless
4. Checks spending across currencies on the train home

### Quote
*"I just want one app that works across all the countries I live in, without surprises on my statement."*

## Persona 2: Marco -- The Crypto-Curious Professional

### Demographics
- **Age:** 34
- **Location:** Milan, Italy
- **Occupation:** Financial Controller at a mid-size manufacturing firm
- **Income:** EUR 72,000/year
- **Languages:** Italian, English
- **Tech Savviness:** High (finance), Medium (crypto)

### Background
Marco has been following crypto since 2020 but has only made a few purchases on centralized exchanges. He finds DeFi interesting but intimidating. He wants to allocate 5-10% of his savings to crypto and stablecoins but does not want to manage multiple wallets, seed phrases, and gas fees. He uses N26 as his primary bank and appreciates its clean interface but finds its crypto offering too limited.

### Goals
- Buy and hold crypto (stablecoins and select tokens) alongside his EUR balance
- Earn yield on stablecoin holdings without managing complex DeFi protocols
- Have everything in one regulated app -- not a separate crypto exchange
- Understand his portfolio in fiat terms at a glance

### Pain Points
- Crypto exchanges feel separate from his "real" banking -- he has to transfer funds back and forth
- Gas fees on Ethereum mainnet are unpredictable and expensive
- DeFi protocols are confusing -- he fears making an irreversible mistake
- Tax reporting for crypto is a nightmare with multiple platforms

### TeslaPay Relevance
- Fuse.io integration with gasless transactions removes the gas fee barrier
- Crypto buy/sell directly from EUR balance -- no external exchange needed
- Solid/soUSD yield-bearing stablecoin provides passive income with minimal complexity
- Single transaction history across fiat and crypto simplifies tax reporting

### Key Scenarios
1. Receives salary, allocates EUR 500 to buy USDC on Fuse network
2. Deposits USDC into Solid soUSD to earn yield while holding stablecoins
3. Reviews combined fiat + crypto portfolio value on dashboard
4. Pays for dinner with Mastercard while crypto holdings appreciate

### Quote
*"I want crypto to be as easy as checking my bank balance. No seed phrases, no gas fee surprises."*

### TeslaPay Relevance
- Multi-currency EUR/PLN accounts with competitive FX rates
- SEPA Instant for fast EUR payments from EU clients

- Transaction export in CSV/PDF for accounting

3. Uses business Mastercard to pay for Adobe Creative Cloud subscription
4. Exports monthly transactions in CSV for her accountant

## Persona 4: Lukas -- The Privacy-Conscious Tech Worker

### Demographics
- **Age:** 37
- **Location:** Vilnius, Lithuania
- **Occupation:** Backend Developer at a cybersecurity firm
- **Income:** EUR 65,000/year
- **Languages:** Lithuanian, English, Russian
- **Tech Savviness:** Very High

### Background
Lukas is deeply aware of data privacy issues through his work. He uses a traditional Lithuanian bank reluctantly and is uncomfortable with how much data big tech neobanks collect. He is an active crypto user who values self-custody but acknowledges that he needs a regulated bank account for salary, bills, and daily spending. He wants the best of both worlds: a regulated EU banking experience with self-custodial crypto capabilities.

### Goals
- A regulated EU bank account with minimal unnecessary data collection
- Self-custodial crypto wallet integrated into his banking app
- Full control over his financial data -- know exactly what is stored and where
- Strong security: hardware key support, certificate pinning, open security audits

### Pain Points
- Big neobanks (Revolut) share data with too many third parties
- Most banking apps have weak security practices (no cert pinning, poor session management)
- Crypto on centralized exchanges means someone else holds his keys
- GDPR data access requests to his current bank take weeks

### TeslaPay Relevance
- Lithuanian-regulated EMI -- data stays in EU, subject to strict GDPR enforcement
- Fuse.io Smart Wallet provides self-custody (keys managed on device)
- Gasless transactions via account abstraction -- usability without custodial compromise
- Strong security architecture with certificate pinning, biometric auth

### Key Scenarios
1. Receives salary via SEPA into TeslaPay EUR account
2. Converts EUR 1,000 to USDC and sends to his Fuse self-custodial wallet
3. Participates in Fuse DeFi protocols directly from the app
4. Reviews GDPR data audit log in app settings

### Quote
*"Not your keys, not your coins. But I also need to pay rent. Give me both in one app."*
